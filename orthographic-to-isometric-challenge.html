Setup

Set up the round

Students get one orthographic question at a time and choose from four isometric options. The wrong answers are designed to be close calls: mirrored, rotated-looking, and almost-right variants.

How it plays

  • Read all three orthographic views before choosing.
  • Drag an isometric card into the answer zone, or tap one to submit it straight away.
  • Distractors are intentionally similar, so students need to compare height changes, mirroring, and missing cubes carefully.

Tip: the fastest-sheet mode is best for competition, while sprint and survival are better for repeated quick practice.
